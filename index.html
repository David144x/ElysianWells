An Honest Reckoning

Something is wrong.
You already know it.

This is not a conspiracy. It is not pessimism. It is the quiet observation that the system most of us were born into — the one that promised a good job, a safe house, a meaningful life in exchange for compliance and productivity — is not delivering on that promise. And it hasn’t been for a long time.

There is no need to stay inside of a broken system and forced to adapt to it’s demands. Elysian Wells was designed for those of us who feel called towards a new way of living, one that is immersed in nature, where food is grown on our land and clean water bubbles up from wells and streams – all without the need for a utility bill or any municipal reliance.

Elysian Wells is not an escape from reality, it is a return to the only one that makes sense.

What we are leaving behind ```

⏰ Someone else's schedule

The alarm. The commute. The meeting that could have been an email. More hours, more output, more consumption — and less time, less rest, less life. The system runs on our exhaustion. It was designed to.

🏭 Food that travelled further than you did

Industrial supply chains, nutritional emptiness dressed up in colorful packaging, GMO and pesticide-laden food grown by strangers, processed by corporations, and delivered with no connection to the land, the season, or the people who produced it.

🚰 Water you have to trust

Municipal treatment, chlorine, fluoride, pharmaceutical residue, and a monthly bill for the privilege. A dependency on systems we cannot inspect, cannot control, and cannot opt out of.

🔑 Renting your life month to month

Belonging contingent on continued payment. A landlord who can raise the price or take it back. No land, no roots, no permanence

📱 Connection on a screen

Algorithms replacing conversation. Notifications replacing presence. The loneliness of a city of millions where no one knows their neighbour’s name.

⚠️ No plan when things go wrong

One power grid. One supply chain. One government. One passport. A life built entirely on the assumption that the systems around us will hold — and no alternative solution for when they don’t.

🧊 Six months of winter

A climate that makes year-round food growing difficult, turns self-sufficiency into a heroic undertaking, and extracts a psychological toll that most people have stopped noticing. Half the year spent waiting for the other half to begin.

📈 Rising costs and shrinking freedoms

Taxes that climb. Regulations that multiply. Cost of living that becomes nonsensical. A government that grows larger and more intrusive with every decade, spending our hard-earned dollars on programs and services that often clash with our ideals and needs.

⌛ Politicians who no longer serve us

Left or right, the pattern is the same — career politicians propping up extractive systems that enriches the few at the expense of the many. Institutions hollowed out. Trust eroded. A social contract designed for a world that no longer exists. The system is not broken. It is working exactly as designed — just not for us.

Most people live inside systems they did not design, eating food they did not grow, drinking water they cannot trace, working for visions that are not their own. The alternative is not ideology — it is architecture. A community designed from first principles around what human beings actually need to flourish: clean food and water produced on site, energy independence, genuine belonging, meaningful work, financial resilience, and the sovereignty to own our days.

Elysian Wells produces coffee, cacao, fruit, vegetables, eggs, honey, medicinal herbs, and eventually shared income from community enterprises. It generates its own power, stores its own water, and functions when outside systems fail. It offers legal residency in a second country, a custom-built home on private land, and a founding circle of eleven other people who chose the lifestyle we are choosing. The result is not a retreat or a surrender to modern extractive systems based on coercision and taxes. It is a complete and self-sustaining alternative to the life most of us are quietly exhausted by.

Why This Place, Specifically

The air, the water,
the food. The body
knows the difference.

Most people living in cities have forgotten what clean air feels like. What spring water tastes like. What it means to eat food that was grown in living volcanic soil and harvested that morning. The body does not forget — it simply adapts, compensates, and quiets its complaints until the contrast becomes impossible to ignore.

The region where Elysian Wells is being established is one of the few places on earth where all three — air, water, and food quality — are exceptional by any measure. The cloud forest produces some of the most oxygenated and particle-free air on the planet. The soil delivers a mineral density that depleted industrial farmland cannot replicate. The spring water naturally delivered in this area requires no treatment, no filtration, no faith in a municipal system to manage.

This is also a region celebrated in longevity research — not because of genetics, but because of the environment. People here live long because the basics of life — air, water, food, community, and purpose — are present and intact in a place of true wild beauty.

Elysian Wells is not a health retreat. But the health outcomes of living here will make themselves obvious. When food, water and energy is secured – when costs are minimal, and when community and connection is present, it isn’t just our bodies getting nourished, our souls and spirit receives the same. This is a toxin-free environment naturally by itself without a 5G tower in sight.
