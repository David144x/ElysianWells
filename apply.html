Before You Apply

This is a serious
endeavour. Beautifully so.

```

If you have read the Home and About pages and something in you recognised what is being described — that recognition is worth taking seriously. Applying is not a commitment. It is a conversation. The selection process exists precisely so that both sides can be certain before anything is signed, paid, or packed.

What is being asked of you is real: five years, ten hours a week, and the willingness to show up for eleven other people the way you want them to show up for you. What you receive in return is also real: land, a home, a community, financial resilience, clean food, clean water, and the rare satisfaction of having built something that will outlast you.

Elysian Wells was not designed for people who are almost ready. It was designed for people who have been ready for a long time and simply haven't found the right thing to say yes to. If that is you — this is it.

🌳 Five-Year Founding Commitment

Founding members commit to a minimum of five years. This is not a retreat, a trial, or an extended holiday. It is the time required to build something that will outlast any one of us. The founding years are the most important years — and they need the founding circle present and committed to see them through.

🤝 Ten Hours Per Week

Every founding member contributes ten hours per week to community life — tending their stewardship role, participating in shared work, and showing up for the community that is showing up for them. The remaining time is entirely your own: your work, your creative life, your exploration. Ten hours is a contribution, not a sacrifice.

🔑 Land Lease Portability at Year Three

Your land lease becomes fully transferable at the beginning of Year Three. The first two years are the foundation — the period when the community most needs its full circle in place. After Year Three, the community stands on its own, and your equity becomes yours to carry. A dignified exit is always available to those who have honoured their commitment.

Most people who feel called to something like this never act on it. The twelve who do will spend the rest of their lives knowing they were the ones who actually chose differently.
```
