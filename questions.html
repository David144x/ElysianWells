Elysian Wells · Founding Member Application

Nineteen questions.
One honest conversation.

These questions are designed not to test you but to give you the space to think clearly about who you are, what you are bringing, and whether this is genuinely the right fit — for you and for the community. There are no right answers. Only honest ones.

19 Questions
5 Sections

Take the time your responses deserve. The quality of your answers is the first thing we will come to know about you. Your responses are sent directly and privately to the founding director upon submission.

Section One

About You

We keep identifying details minimal at this stage. Full legal details are requested only at the membership agreement stage, following a successful selection process.

Section Two

Motivation & Readiness

These questions distinguish genuine readiness from a well-intentioned impulse. The quality of your reflection matters more than the content of your résumé.

01
What brings you to Elysian Wells specifically, and how long have you been thinking about a life change of this kind?Distinguishes impulse from genuine readiness.
02
What does your current life look like, and what specifically are you leaving behind?Reveals whether you have thought honestly about the transition, or are romanticising an escape.
03
Have you ever lived communally, travelled extensively, or lived outside your home country? What did you learn?Prior experience with discomfort, novelty, and shared living is one of the strongest predictors of success.
04
What does your support network think about this decision?Tells us whether you are making a considered choice — and whether your relationships are strong enough to survive the disagreement.
Section Three

Skills & Contribution

A community of twelve runs on the real competencies of the people inside it. These questions reveal what you bring — and what you are still learning.

05
Which stewardship role resonates most with you, and what specific experience or skills make you suited for it?The most important practical question on this form. Read the eleven roles carefully before responding.
06
What can you build, grow, fix, or make with your hands?List it specifically — tools you can use, things you have made, skills you have built over time.
07
Describe a time you contributed to something larger than yourself. What was your role and what did you learn about how you show up for others?The specifics of the example matter more than its scale.
08
What skills are you hoping to develop at Elysian Wells that you don't currently have?Shows self-awareness and genuine appetite for growth.
Section Four

Character & Conflict

Community life surfaces conflict inevitably. These questions reveal emotional maturity more reliably than almost any other measure. Answer them honestly.

09
Describe the most difficult interpersonal conflict you have navigated. How did you handle it and what would you do differently?The single most revealing question on this form.
10
What are your edges — the conditions or behaviours in others that you find genuinely difficult to be around?Someone who claims to have no edges is either dishonest or unexamined.
11
How do you respond when you strongly disagree with a group decision?Reveals your relationship to authority and collective process.
Section Five

Practical Commitment

These questions address the practical realities of the transition. Financial transparency protects both the applicant and the community.

12
What is your current financial situation, and how are you planning to fund the founding investment and your ongoing life at Elysian Wells?Full financial documentation is provided to all applicants before any funds are committed.
13
Are you applying alone or with a partner? If with a partner, how aligned are you both on this decision?Both people need to want this independently and genuinely.
14
What does your ideal day look like at Elysian Wells three years in — specifically, how are you spending your time beyond the ten hours of community contribution?The specificity of the answer is itself diagnostic.
Section Six

Values & Vision

The quality of reflection in your responses here is the most important signal of all.

15
Which of the seven founding values — truth, beauty, compassion, wisdom, prosperity, justice, freedom — do you find most natural to live by, and which do you find most challenging?Both answers matter equally.
16
Why do you believe intentional communities fail, and what would you personally do to prevent that outcome here?Someone who has studied this history and thought hard about failure modes is exactly who this is for.
17
What does financial sovereignty mean to you, and how are you currently working toward it?Reveals whether the applicant has thought seriously about economic independence.
18
Is there anything about your life, health, history, or circumstances that the founding director should know before making a decision?Honesty here is treated as a sign of character. What is not disclosed and discovered later is a different matter entirely.
19
If you could ask the founding director of Elysian Wells one question, what would it be?The quality of the question tells us as much about you as any answer you have given.

When you are ready, press submit below. Your responses will open in your email client, fully formatted and labelled, addressed to the founding director. Take a moment to review them before sending — this is your application.

Please enter your name and email address before submitting. Your information is held in strict confidence. Location details disclosed only to accepted members.
